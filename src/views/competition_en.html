Photo contest WikiDaheim

Present your home town with your pictures on Wikipedia

Help Wikipedia illustrate villages and areas of Austria with your pictures and show unique features of those areas!

Timeframe

You are able to upload pictures (and other media like video and audio) to Wikimedia Commons at any time via the upload links on this website. To take part in the next photo contest your media have to be uploaded from January 1st through October 5th 2025, 24:00 CEST. This is also the timeframe for all special prizes except for the international Wiki Loves Monuments photo competition, where images will only be accepted if uploaded at or after September 6th 2025.

Rules

General

  • You can only upload media to Wikimedia Commons, if you have a user account (create one, if you don't have one) and if you're logged in with your user account.
  • You will also have to be able to receive emails, which you can set in your preferences via the option "Allow other users to email me" on Wikimedia Commons). Otherwise it won't be possible to contact you when you have won a prize.
  • The picture / video / audio has to have been taken in Austria and by the uploading person.
  • The pictures / videos / audios must be uploaded during the contest period stated above.
  • Media can either be taken during the contest or prior to the contest.
  • For pictures of works of art that are only temporarily in the public space and for pictures of interiors the general rules of Commons apply (Copyright rules by territory/Austria).
  • For media depicting people keep in mind their personality rights (Photographs of identifiable people).
  • Media that were created with technical support, for example a drone, need to be proven to have been taken legally.
  • Documenting the important aspects is more important than artistic aspects.
  • Media have to be uploaded with a free Creative Commons license on Wikimedia Commons.
  • Describing and adding categories to your media would be very helpful for everyone looking for your media afterwards. Description and categories that are proposed during the upload process can be improved. Location info (coordinates) of the object and / or of the camera location are exremly supportive.
  • There is no limitation to storage, and thus no need to downgrade media in size or quality. Preferred is the best quality available.

Photos

  • The resolution must be at least 2 Megapixel.
  • Sets of images, that show objects with only minimal differences in perspective, are not that valuable for Wikipedia compared to different views of an object. It is recommended that you make select the best in a set before uploading.

Videos

  • Only free video formats are supported for upload. Supported video formats are (with file suffix): Ogg (.ogv), WebM (.webm), MPEG-1/MPEG-2 (.mpg, .mpeg). Non-free formats have to be converted to free formats before uploading. See details at Commons:File types.

Audio files

  • Only free audio formats are supported for upload. Supported audio formats are (with file suffix): Ogg (.ogv), WebM (.webm), MPEG-1/MPEG-2 (.mpg, .mpeg). Non-free formats have to be converted to free formats before uploading. For mp3 files you need special privileges, which will not be granted in context of the contest. See details at Commons:File types.
  • Wir recommend a sampling rate of 44.1 kHz and a resolution of 16 bit or higher.

Media, that do not satisfy the requirements can be marked with Commons-Category:Media from WikiDaheim 2025 in Austria questioned by prejury by the prejury and thus will be temporarily excluded from the contest. In parallel authors / uploaders will be contacted on their user talk page and will get the opportunity to fix the mentioned problems.

Is there something I should keep in mind when uploading my pictures?

Pictures should adhere to the policies and guidelines of Wikimedia Commons. Large changes or manipulations of your picture, including visible watermarks should not be added. The picture should also have a minimum resolution of 2 megapixels. The image guidelines can help you determine what your best options are when uploading your picture.

For Wikipedia and its sister projects the most valuable photos are those, which show still undocumented objects or which show a greatly changed condition or a completely new aspect of an object (help filling the gaps). However, in the contest the photos are rated only after their photographical quality.

You can upload media during the competition and exclude them from taking part in the contest by adding category Media from WikiDaheim 2025 in Austria not for prejury. To do so is recommended e.g. for pictures with much more documentary value than photographical beauty (e.g. signs explaining an object), or pictures you do not want to put to the jury process.

Uploading pictures using wikidaheim.at or using the upload from the lists in Wikipedia will automatically nominate these pictures for the photo contest. However, if you as an experienced user prefer to upload pictures directly to Commons, or are using a different tool set, you have to mark these pictures with {{WikiDaheim|2024}} and the suitable topic to nominate for the contest. Also don't forget to add the correct ids for the object, if any.

Jury

A prejury will preselect the images and the best 500 pictures will be submitted to the actual jury, which will decide on the winners in early November. To allow a broad participation of many users, the number of images per user passed to the jury is limited to the 10 best images in the prejury result.
