Photo contest WikiDaheim

Present your home town with your pictures on Wikipedia!

Help Wikipedia illustrate villages and areas of Austria with your pictures and show unique features of those areas! The 2018 edition of the competition ended on October 7th 2018!

Timeframe

You can upload pictures to Wikimedia Commons from July 1st through October 7th, 24:00 CEST via the upload links on this website.

Prizes for 2018

sponsored by Wikimedia Austria
1st prize: 600 € voucher for photography equipment or a trip to Wikimania 2019 in Sweden
2nd prize: 150 € voucher for photography equipment
3rd prize: 75 € voucher for photography equipment
4th through 10th prize: 50 € voucher for photography equipment

Special prizes
1st prize: 50 € voucher for photography equipment for the following topics:

  • Monuments (in cooperation with the Federal Monuments Office
  • Nature (protected areas of Austria like national parks, natural monuments, etc.)
  • Common infrastructure (infrastructure listed on WikiDaheim like cemeteries, museums, schools, etc.)
  • Public art (not only the art listed on WikiDaheim, but all art found in public spaces)
  • Cellar streets

Rules

  • The picture has to have been taken in Austria.
  • The pictures must be uploaded during the contest period stated above.
  • The picture can either be taken during the contest or prior to the contest.
  • Documenting the important aspects is more important than artistic aspects.
  • Pictures have to be uploaded with a free Creative Commons license on Wikimedia Commons.
  • Describing and adding categories to your picture would be very helpful for everyone looking for your picture afterwards.
  • You will also have to be able to receive emails, which you can set in your preferences via the option "Allow other users to email me". Otherwise it won't be possible to contact you when you have won a prize!

Is there something I should keep in mind when uploading my pictures?

Pictures should adhere to the policies and guidelines of Wikimedia Commons. Large changes or manipulations of your picture, including visible watermarks should not be added. The picture should also have a minimum resolution of 2 megapixels. The image guidelines can help you determine what your best options are when uploading your picture.

Jury

A prejury selected the images and the best 500 pictures were submitted to the actual jury, which picked the winning pictures in late November.
